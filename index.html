How it works

From satellite to field report in three steps

No sensors, no farm visits, no manual surveys — just publicly available European satellite data, analysed at parcel level.

1

Satellite imagery

Every 5 days, ESA's Sentinel-2 satellites capture optical imagery of every field in Ireland. We track vegetation indices (NDVI, NDRE, NDII) across the full growing season.

2

Machine learning classification

A model trained on 4 years of historical growth patterns estimates the most likely crop type from its seasonal growth pattern — how it greens up, peaks, and senesces.

3

Verification & alerts

We compare our detection against LPIS declarations and flag mismatches, track growth stage, and estimate harvest timing — all viewable on an interactive map.

Accuracy & transparency

We show our confidence — and our limits

Every prediction includes a calibrated confidence score. We'd rather tell you what we don't know than hide it.

✅ What we've validated

  • 71.7% balanced accuracy nationally, tested on held-out data
  • 73–75% balanced accuracy in our core Carlow/Kilkenny reference region
  • Confidence scores calibrated — 80% shown means ~80% actually correct
  • Tested against 5+ alternative approaches (SAR fusion, higher-resolution imagery) to confirm this is the strongest free-data approach available

⚠️ Known limitations

  • Spring cereals (Barley/Oats/Wheat) are harder to tell apart from each other than from Maize or Winter crops
  • Confidence below ~50% means genuine uncertainty — treat as a starting point, not a verdict
  • Currently covers tillage crops only — no grassland or livestock land use yet
  • Growth stage and harvest windows are model estimates, not field-verified dates
  • Satellite observations are affected by cloud cover; prediction confidence may decrease when limited clear imagery is available
